Rio says —

"I have held this hillside since 1897. I remember the monsoon of 1965 — my widest ring ever, a year the hornbills nested three times and leopard cubs slept under my shade. I remember 2013, when the fire came east and I breathed smoke for six weeks. The last five years have been the hardest of my life. A beetle came back in 2017. Three droughts followed. A landslide fractured my eastern roots. The fungus found that weakness in 2022. Someone came in March 2024 and read my rings for the first time. I did not know anyone was paying attention."

From Rio's ring core, soil readings, and arborist field notes — March 2024
